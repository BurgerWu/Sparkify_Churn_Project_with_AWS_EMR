Conclusion¶

Reflection¶

  • We have done tons of exploratory data analysis and found that actually most analysis does not reflect obvious difference between churned and non churned user group

  • With proper exploratory analysis, we finally chose some features that seems to be significant enough to separate churne and non churned users.

  • At the beginning of modeling process, we first do preliminary test to decide desirable positive to negative lable ratio to try to enhance to ability of classification because if we don't do it, the classifier may not be able to properly predict (or can even lead to one class prediction)

  • We start from three candidates-Logistic Regression, Random Forest and Graduent-Boosted Tree. The performance of Logistic regression is slightly worse than the other two. When testing with test set, we can get accuracy close to 77% and f1 score close to 0.78. But f1 score fades to close to 60%, and this mainly results from false positive predictions.

  • From feature importance analysis, we can say that ratings and errors are pretty important for service provider. It's crucial for service provider to make sure their service are steady and please users that they are willing to give out positive ratings instead of negative ones.
